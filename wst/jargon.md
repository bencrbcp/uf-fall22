# Gender Studies Jargon

* Sex: A label applied to body based on __biological determinants__ i.e. genitals, chromosomes, hormone levels.
    - The main 3 categories are female, male, and intersex (from 0.018% to 1.7%)
    - Cannot be changed

* Gender: A __pesronal label__ used to denote how a person identifies on a feminine to masculine spectrum.
    - Most often infants are assigned one of two binary genders at birth

* Sexual orientation: A __personal label__ used to defin the categories of people someone is physicaly attracted to.

* Cisgender

* Transgender

* Cis-normativity

* Gender polarization: The notion that femininity and masculinity are polar-opposites.
    -

* Essentialism: Doctrine that at least some things have a common nature.
    - A fixed, and unchanging property or set of properties that necessarily make something or some organism what it is.

Romantic Identity / Orientation: A personal label used to define the categories of people someone is romantically attracted to

LGBTQIA: Acronym

Queer: A label used to describe a wide variety of sexual and/or romantic identities within LGBT.
    - Recent trend of using it as an umbrella term for LGBT
    - History of being used as a slur...
    - ...should only be used to describe people who explicitly express this as their identity.

Privilege: An advantage a person has or embodies based on certain identities or statuses that grant them power and agency within a social structure.
    - Having one or multiple privileged identities/statuses does not mean a person will have no problems in life...
    - ...but rather that those particular identities/statuses will not be the source for their problems.
    - e.g. in American society: male, cis, hetero, white, able-bodied, neurotypical, middle class and above, formally educated, etc.

* Infradian rhythm
    - In chronobiology, an infradian rhythm is a rhythm with a period longer than the period of a circadian rhythm, i.e., with a frequency of less than one cycle in 24 hours


## Patriarchal Bargain

"A decision by females to accept gender rules that disadvantage women in exchange for whatever power one can wrest from the system" (Wade, 2010)
    - In such bargains, an individual woman benefits at the expense of women generally
    - i.e. woman who gains celebrity status and money by being a sex object, but by doing so furthers the objectification of women who are then disadvantaged
